How to Help Your Dog Cope with Fireworks Anxiety

The sound of fireworks sends countless dogs into a tailspin of terror. The unfamiliar sounds trigger the dog's sympathetic nervous system, leading to responses like fright, flight, or freeze. When a dog's anxious reaction is not dealt with, the dog will become sensitive to random noises. When anxiety kicks in, it's important to formulate a strategy to handle your fur baby's discomfort and fear.
To Minimise Fireworks Anxiety in Dogs:

  • Create a Safe Space
  • Desensitization Training
  • Provide Distractions
  • Use Calming Aids
  • Close Windows and Curtains


Cat Litter Box Tips - Do's & Don'ts of Litter

DO

  • Scoop waste daily.
  • Clean litter boxes regularly.
  • Refill the litter box with fresh litter.
  • Provide enough boxes.

DON'T

  • Flush litter clumps down the toilet.
  • Use cleaning products with ammonia.
  • Neglect the boxes.
  • Place the boxes in undesirable areas.


Hamsters Need Large, Secure Homes

Hamsters is often viewed as prey for larger animals, therefore it may be attacked by companion animals in the home if proper precautions aren't taken. To help prevent other animals from harming them, it's imperative that hamsters have a safe, spacious, and sturdy enclosure.

To protect hamsters
☑ Barred cage space NO BIGGER than hald an inch.
☑ Cage IS NOT within human arm's reach of loose fabrics like curtains or clothing.
☑ Put something HEAVY over the top of your cage.
